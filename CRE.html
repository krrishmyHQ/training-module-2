Conventional Leasing & Operator Model · How Space Actually Gets Transacted
The Original Model · Still the Largest
Conventional Leasing
Direct landlord-to-tenant. Long-term. Nothing bundled. The tenant negotiates every clause, pays CAM separately, and is responsible for fitout. It's how the majority of Grade A stock in India is still leased.
How long?
Usually 3–5 years. Leaving before that typically means paying a few months of rent as a penalty.
Does the rent go up?
Yes — usually 15% every 3 years, or 5% annually on newer deals.
Upfront deposit?
6–12 months' rent, paid before moving in. Refunded at the end of the lease.
Monthly extras?
Building maintenance, power, security — charged separately on top of the base rent.
Setting up the office?
The company pays for all interiors and furniture. On large deals, the building owner may contribute some of this cost.
The Model Behind Every Flex Space
How Operators Work
A flex operator takes a full floor or building from a developer, furnishes and equips it, then offers individual offices and desks to companies on flexible monthly plans. They are both a tenant (to the building owner) and a service provider (to their clients).
The Simple Economics
Charge clients more per seat than it costs to provide that seat.
The operator pays the building owner a fixed monthly amount. They then divide the space into individual seats and private suites and charge their clients a higher rate per seat. When the space is mostly full, the business works well. When it sits empty, it doesn't. That pressure to stay occupied is exactly why operators value sales partners who bring them clients.
Why This Matters to You
Every space on myHQ's platform is run by an operator who needs it to stay occupied. When you bring a client to a space, you are solving a real business problem for the operator — and building a relationship on both sides. The more you understand how operators think, the better you'll be at matching the right client to the right space.

The 2030 View · Where This Market Is Going
India CRE · 2024 to 2030 · Click each milestone to expand
The market you're entering will be 3x larger by decade's end
2024
India's best-ever office year.
72M sq ft
Net absorption. Third consecutive year above 60M. IT/ITeS + GCCs + BFSI drove demand. Flex hit 18% market share.
JLL India Q4 2024

Bengaluru alone absorbed 20M+ sq ft — more than most entire countries. Three flex operators listed or filed IPOs: Awfis (listed, 4.5x oversubscribed), Smartworks (DRHP filed), IndiQube (DRHP filed). The sector is past "emerging" — it is emerged. Every market signal from 2024 points to continued momentum through 2026. Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Pune are absorbing supply as fast as developers can deliver it.

2025
GCC surge. 1,700+ captives target 50M sq ft.
$46B
Annual GCC revenue from India. JPMorgan, Goldman, Boeing, Apple — all expanding aggressively. Every GCC is a potential 50K–500K sq ft requirement.
NASSCOM GCC 2024

India is now the default destination for any Fortune 500 company building offshore technology or finance capability. Indian talent at 25–30% of US cost, in buildings that meet global ESG standards. This calculus will not change in your career. GCC demand is structural, not cyclical. By 2025, India will house more GCC employees than any country outside the US. The expansion continues regardless of global macro headwinds — because the value arbitrage is too strong to ignore.

2026
Flex market hits ₹32,000 Cr — triple 2021.
3x
CBRE projection. Operators building supply ahead of demand — 40+ new centres planned in Delhi NCR, Mumbai, and Bengaluru alone.
CBRE India Flex 2024

Enterprise flex — managed offices for 50–500 people — grew 80% between 2021 and 2024. Every corporate that went hybrid now has a "flex-first" policy in their real estate playbook. The operators who built scale during 2020–22 are now the publicly-listed companies. This is the inflection point: the clients who once asked "can we use flex?" are now asking "which flex product is right for our scale?" That shift is what creates deal volume for you.
